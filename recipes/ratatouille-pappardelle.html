Ratatouille Pappardelle

Summer vegetables find a splendid home among ribbons of pappardelle accented with freshly shaved Parmesan for an easy pasta dish that's also fun to pronounce.

Prep Time
30 mins
Cook Time
40 mins
Servings
4

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil for ratatouille
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil for pasta
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb tomatoes, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 eggplant, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 3 zucchini, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons herbes de Provence
  • salt and cracked black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup dry red wine
  • 4 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 ounces Parigiano-Reggiano cheese, shaved
  • 2 teaspoons balsamic vinegar, or to taste (optional)

Steps

  1. Pour olive oil into a large pot over high heat.
  2. Add onion and garlic and saute for 2 minutes.
  3. Reduce heat and add tomatoes, eggplant, zucchini, bell pepper, tomato sauce, herbes de Provence, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stir in wine, cover, and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Uncover and simmer until vegetables are just tender, 12 to 15 minutes.
  6. Leave ratatouille warm in the pot until ready to use.
  7. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil.
  8. Cook pasta at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, about 10 minutes.
  9. Drain, reserving 1/2 cup cooking water.
  10. Toss pasta with oil.
  11. Reheat ratatouille in an extra-large skillet over medium heat.
  12. Add pasta and spinach; toss just until spinach wilts.
  13. Add enough reserved pasta water to make a saucy consistency.
  14. Season with salt and pepper.
  15. Sprinkle with Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese and drizzle with balsamic vinegar.
